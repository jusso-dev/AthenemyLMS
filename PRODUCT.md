# Athenemy Product Context

## Register

product

## Product Purpose

Athenemy is a modern self-hostable LMS for creators, teams, and small organisations. It helps instructors and admins create, sell, deliver, and track online courses while keeping ownership of their data and infrastructure choices.

## Primary Users

- Course creators who need a practical authoring workflow without enterprise LMS clutter.
- Instructors who publish lessons, assessments, certificates, and learner resources.
- Organisation admins who manage members, roles, invitations, starter courses, portals, automations, integrations, billing, and setup.
- Learners who need clear course navigation, readable lesson content, progress feedback, and certificates.
- Self-hosting operators who need explicit integration states for Clerk, Supabase, Stripe, R2, and email.
- Developers who need scoped API access and webhook contracts for integrations.

## Brand Personality

Calm, capable, trustworthy, and direct. The product should feel like a serious learning operations tool, not a marketing template or a raw admin panel.

## Product Principles

- Learning content comes first: navigation, chrome, and setup messaging should support the learner's task without competing with the lesson.
- Creator workflows should feel guided, reversible, and familiar to people who have used modern course platforms.
- Operational clarity beats decoration: billing, organisation management, publishing, and import flows should show state and next steps plainly.
- Familiar LMS affordances should be executed with modern polish: course outlines, progress, lesson players, assessments, invites, and settings should feel predictable.
- New organizations should have a useful starting point through editable default courses instead of an empty LMS shell.
- Integration and automation features should keep Athenemy state first-party and inspectable, even when external services execute background work.
- Dark and light themes must preserve the integrity of imported course content, especially slide imagery and screenshots.
- Empty, loading, error, and setup states are part of the product experience, not placeholders.

## Anti-References

- Moodle-style clutter, dense controls without hierarchy, and page furniture that distracts from the course.
- Generic SaaS card grids, decorative dashboards, and marketing-like hero composition inside authenticated workflows.
- Raw CRUD screens that expose implementation details instead of user tasks.
- Dark-mode treatments that make uploaded slides, transparent images, diagrams, or screenshots hard to read.
- Hidden failure states, crashes for validation errors, or setup notices that look visually detached from the rest of the app.

## Accessibility And Quality Bar

The target is a production-ready MVP with a flagship feel on the core learning and course creation paths. Interfaces should meet WCAG AA contrast, support keyboard navigation, keep focus states visible, respect reduced motion, and use semantic structure for lessons, forms, navigation, and status messages.
